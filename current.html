David Lieberman

Bernstein’s Inequality for Certain Singular Rings

February 8th
Bernstein’s Inequality is a classic result of Joseph Bernstein (also spelled I. N. Bernstein) about D-modules: If M is a module over the ring of differential operators on a polynomial ring in n variables, then the dimension of M is at least n and no more than 2n. In this talk, we will see some of the work that I have done with Jack Jeffries to prove this result for some non-polynomial (aka singular) rings. In particular we will see that the inequality holds for D-modules of the degree 2 Veronese subring of K[x,y]. No advanced algebraic knowledge will be required, but a good disposition and a can-do attitude will be useful skills for learning something new from this talk.

Frank Zimmitti

Bezout's theorem in higher dimensions

February 15th
Bezout's theorem is a famous result in projective geometry which allows you to compute the number of points of intersection of two curves in the projective plane. With some work, this result generalizes nicely to intersections of varieties in higher dimensional projective spaces. We will briefly discuss the classical version of Bezout's theorem and introduce some fundamental ideas in algebraic geometry in order to be able to state the theorem for projective spaces of any dimension. No advanced knowledge of algebraic geometry will be assumed.
